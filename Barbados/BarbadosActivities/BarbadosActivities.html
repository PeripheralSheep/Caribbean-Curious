What You Can Do in Barbados

Barbados has a lot to offer for tourists when it comes to exploration. It can range from visiting a local Cricket match to exploring the fauna and flora of Barbados to even watching racing at Bushy Park. There's so much to do in Barbados and we will cover some areas of interest you can do.

Cricket

If you are a fan of cricket then look no further. Apart from Kensington Oval, there are a bunch of cricket pitches in Barbados. From March to November in a typical, COVID-19 free year, cricket matches are played on these pitches usually on the weekends.

These matches can range from school cricket matches to club division level. Clubs such as: Counterpoint Wanderers, Empire Cricket Club and Carlton compete in the top-level, the Elite Division, during this period.

Marine Tours

From Kayak Shipwreck Tours to Catamaran Snorkeling Cruises to Submarine Tours, Barbados has a lot of options just off its shores.

When on these tours, there is the chance to witness the beauty of Barbadian Coral Reefs and the wildlife they are home to.

There's no one place to go to enjoy these tours but trip advisor offers a lot of information regarding tours such as prices and length of excursions.

St. Lawrence Gap

St. Lawrence Gap is one of the most popular tourist locations in Barbados. It is 1.3km long, one-way road located in the parish of Christ Church. It is commonly referred to as 'The Gap'

From the time you enter the gap there are restaurants, hotels and shopping areas there at your fingertips. Be sure to also visit at night to obtain the nightlife experience.

Totally Barbados does a wonderful job on giving more information on St. Lawrence Gap so make sure to visit there.

Harrison's Cave

One of Barbados.org's 'Seven Wonders of Barbados', Harrison's Cave is a must go. First known records of the Cave are from 1796 but it was re-discovered and mapped in 1970 by Danish Ole Sorenson and Barbadian Anthony Mason.

The beautiful limestone cave is full of stalactites, stalagmites and streams of clear running water which come from waterfalls to form beautiful, clear pools.

Tours to Harrison Cave can be a Tram Ride (which is the popular option), an eco-adventure tour, or a walk-in cave tour. Visit their official website to learn more about these tours.

The Barbados Wildlife Reserve

Barbados may not have a zoo but it does have a wildlife reserve. It is located in St. Peter and is opposite Farley Hill National Park.

You have the opportunity to stroll leisurely through the reserve and watch the animals in their natural environment. Animals such as deer, peacocks, green monkeys, turtles and iguanas.

A trip to the reserve may be one of your lengthier rides while in Barbados but it is worth it. Read more about it here.

Crop Over Festival

This is the largest festival that one can experience in Barbados. Having roots from celebrating the end of the harvest season during the plantation era, It runs from June to the first Monday in August.
Throughout this season, there are:

  • Calypso Competitons and Concerts
  • Soca Competitions and Concerts
  • Costume Competitions
  • Bridgetown Market
  • Cultural Exhibitions
    • and finally
  • Grand Kadooment Day to mark off the end of the festival

Grand Kadooment Day is like Carnival in the other Caribbean countries. Revellers parade in their costumes with bands along a pre-determined route. Learn more about Crop Over festical on the National Cultural Foundation's Website.

Oistins Fish Fry

Oistins is an active fishing town located on the southside of Barbados. Every Friday night, there is a fish fry at Oistins Bay Gardens which serves swordfish, marlin, tuna, flying fish and lobster in a very informal setting. Depending on the vendor, you can get your fish grilled or fried right infront of your eyes.

Food is available from 7:00pm and by 8:00pm the area might be crowded. If you decide to go before 7:00pm., there is the opportunity to collect fish scraps from vendors and feed the turtles.
