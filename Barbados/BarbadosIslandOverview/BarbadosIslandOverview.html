Welcome to the Gem of The Caribbean Sea

Welcome to the webpages dedicated to Barbados. Barbados is the most easterly of all the Caribbean Islands and spans across 166 square miles. The people of Barbados are officially known as 'Barbadians' but you are more likely to hear them being referred to as 'Bajans' which is more popular.

Barbados is sub-divided into 11 districts called parishes. these 11 parishes are St. Michael, St. James, St. Joseph, St. John, St. Thomas, St. George, Christ Church, St. Philip, St. Andrew, St. Peter and St. Lucy. The map shown shows the shape of Barbados and where each of these parishes are located.

Barbados then has 4 main towns: Bridgetown (Capital), Oistins, Speightstown, and Holetown.

located in St. Michael, Bridgetown is the largest town in Barbados. Bridgetown and its Garrison is a UNESCO World Heritage Site. The Jewish Museum is located here, Independence Square, which is similar to Trafalgar Square, is located here with a statue of the first Prime Minister of Barbados, Errol Barrow.

Holetown is a tourist hotspot located in St.James and has access to the beautiful West Coast of Barbados. Many tourists stay in the hotels and villas located in this area

Oistins is a fish town located in Christ Church and many of the inviduals living in this area catch and sell fish. Oistins Fish Fry also occurs here every Friday night.

Speightstown is located in St. Peter. It was once named Jamestown and historically was a port. Now, Speightstown which balances colonial charm with modern touristic sites.
