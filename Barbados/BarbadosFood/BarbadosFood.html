One Stop Guide to Bajan Cuisine

Barbados is home to a number of tasty foods which were developed from their time as a colony of England.

Famous foods include:
  • Fishcakes
  • Bakes
  • Conkies
  • Cou-Cou and Flying Fish
  • Pudding and Souse
  • Macaroni Pie

Fishcakes

These are a traditional breakfast item which are often served on their own, or with a baked good called bakes

They can be found as appetizers in restaurants, finger food at cocktails parties or even sandwiched between 'salt bread' to make a bajan famous Bread and Two

They are made with salted code, fresh seasonings such as parsley and thyme, baking powder, water and hot pepper if you're feeling dicey.

Bakes

Bakes aren't strictly a Bajan food but the way Bajans make them certainly is different.

Bajans add sugar to their bakes to make them sweet and savoury. They are typically made with sugar, whatever and flour as the main ingredients but other ingredients are added to please tastebuds

If you are interested in how to make these sweet delights, click here to learn how to cook them.

Conkies

A Conkie is a popular cultural food that is often eaten in the month of November to celebrate Barbados' Independence. Some Bajans may eat these all year round however.

They are made with corn, pumpkin, coconut and sweet potato and are cooked wrapped in green banana leaves.

They are a bit labour intensive to make but if you are interested in making conkies, feel free to learn.

Cou-Cou and Flying Fish

Cou-Cou and Flying Fish make up the National dish of Barbados. Cou-Cou is a cornmeal and okra product while Flying Fish is the National Fish of Barbados.

The Cou-Cou is stirred continuously with a Cou-Cou stick, which has a resemblance to a small cricket bat. It has to be stirred vigorously to ensure that a smooth, fluffy meal is made.

Take part in Bajan culture! learn how to create the National Dish of Barbados

Pudding and Souse

Pudding and Souse is a traditional Saturday lunch in Barbados. The pudding is essentially steamed sweet potato while the Souse is pickled pork.

Many locals go to food places and vendors to buy their portion such as The Villar Bar at Lemon Arbor in St. John

If you want to try making your own Pudding and Souse instead, take a look at this article

Macaroni Pie

Macaroni Pie is for certain going to be at every special occasion or picnic in Barbados. Arguably the most popular food, Macaroni Pie is a bajan take to Macaroni and Cheese.

Instead of elbow macaroni, it is made with macaroni tubes and then baked. As one may guess, cheese is also a key ingredient.
